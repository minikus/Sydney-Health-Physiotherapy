Sport Performance: Control and Efficiency of Movements

In the CBD, we are trying to Balance our busy Work Schedule with Intense Workout routines and Organized sports. Amongst the Most Popular here in the CBD: Yoga, Pilates, Gym class, Lunch-time Touch Footy, Soccer, Pole Dancing.

Muscle tightness, weakness, and joint stiffness can all contribute to Non-Optimal Biomechanics (movements) and impede on sport performance and leading to Repetitive Strain Injury.

To be able to perform optimally, it is important to focus on control and efficiency of your movements. This means looking into whether the correct muscles are firing? Is it firing timely? Is there enough joint play? What is causing restrictions to movements? What kind of speed and agility is required to perform well? These are the issues we investigate during physical assessments here in Sydney Health Physio CBD.

We encourage Repetitive Practice of Simple Building-Block movements (such as Squats in gym trainers and Trunk Rotation in dancers) in Optimal Form. In the case of Squats, muscles such as the VMO and Gluteus Max should activate timely. With practice, a building block movement such as well-controlled Squats can transfer to better performance of a more complexed task such as running or jumping.

The idea of physiotherapy exercises when it comes to “Control” is for you to work on those simple movements Consciously so that optimal movements and muscle firing happens during the actual sport Automatically. The essence of better performance is movement efficiency. Sydney Health Physio CBD focuses on giving your body the option for more free flowing movements in the aim of being more injury free.
