Neck Pain

Living in a civilized and advanced society, we are often required to commit to one posture for long hours. Many of us are engaged to our phones, monitors, or documents for hours. Sitting in front of the desk for long periods of time leads to the development of what we call an “Upper Cross” posture. This type of sitting posture is often the driver of Neck pain, Shoulder pain, and Headache.

Upper Cross Posture: "habitual poked out chin-rounded shoulder" posture that comes hand in hand with long hours in the office, can result in Adaptive Shortening of the Suboccipital muscles. It also causes the cervical spine to change alignment resulting in increased stress of the facet joints and the posterior discs. The position may also lead to Weakness of the Deep Neck Flexors muscles, the Rhomboids, the Serratus muscles, and the Lower Trapezius. Office workers with poor posture also develops Tightness in the Pectoralis muscles and the Levator Scapulae muscles (tightness on the side of the neck). This muscle imbalance leads to compressive loading on your neck, causing cervical articular joint locking and possible disc issues.

Carrying an upper cross posture for long periods of time causes bone decay, headaches, migraines, anxiety, sleep deprivation, and even depression.

Useful tips to rejuvenate and maintain overworked neck and upper back:

  1. Regular change in posture
  2. Having an individualized Ergonomic set-up
  3. Do controlled stretches and movements for your chest, neck, as well as the lateral trunk muscles.
  4. Stress relief such as meditation
  5. Sydney Health Physio CBD provides Regular Maintenance Soft Tissue Therapy to keep your spine in the best possible alignment.
